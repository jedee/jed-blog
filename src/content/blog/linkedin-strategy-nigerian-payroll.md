# LinkedIn Content Strategy for Nigerian Payroll Tech

*What to post, when to post, and what not to do.*

---

## The Core Insight

NSITB compliance is a niche within a niche. Your audience isn't every Nigerian startup founder — it's:
- **HR managers** at companies with 50–500 employees
- **Payroll officers** at government parastatals and agencies
- **Founders of HR software companies** building payroll features
- **Finance/Accounting professionals** who deal with payroll tax queries

Each group has a different trigger point. HR managers care about audit-ready numbers. Payroll officers care about NSITB rules changing. Software founders care about integration API docs. Finance professionals care about tax liability.

Your LinkedIn strategy should address each of these, in order.

---

## Content Pillars (Post in This Ratio)

**40% — Technical Depth (Playbook Posts)**
Show the actual calculation logic, the edge cases you handled, the NSITB bracket table you extracted from source documents. This is credibility content. A payroll officer who sees your bracket breakdown and recognizes the exact format NSITB uses will screenshot and share it.

Format: Code snippet + explanation of why this edge case matters. No more than 150 words of body text per post.

**30% — Founder Story (Relatability Posts)**
Why you built it, what went wrong in the first version, what Finance caught during manual verification. This is human content — it makes the technical credibility feel earned, not just proclaimed.

Format: Short story + one lesson. The technical detail is secondary; the lesson is primary.

**20% — Industry Observations (Positioning Posts)**
Nigerian payroll is a known pain point with low public awareness of solutions. Comment on PAYE changes, NHF cap adjustments, NSITB enforcement trends. This establishes you as someone who follows this space closely.

Format: Observation → what it means for your audience → question to prompt replies.

**10% — Product Announcements (Launch Posts)**
New versions, npm milestones, documentation updates. Short, factual, with a clear link to what it means for the user.

---

## Posting Cadence

**Minimum:** 3 posts per week. LinkedIn's algorithm rewards consistent creators over occasional high-performers.

**Optimal:** 5 posts per week (Mon–Fri, 8–10am WAT). Nigerian professionals are most active between 8am and noon.

**What not to do:** Don't batch-post and disappear. The algorithm and your audience both expect regularity.

---

## The LinkedIn Post Formulas That Work for Technical B2B

**Formula 1: The Code + Context Post**
```
Hook (1 line): "Most Nigerian payroll systems get the NHF cap wrong."

Code snippet (3–5 lines of the calculation)

Explanation (2-3 sentences on why this matters)

CTA: "Check your system — the cap is ₦7,500/month, not ₦12,500."
```

**Formula 2: The Verification Post**
```
Hook: "Finance caught an error in our first version."

Story (3-4 sentences — what was wrong, how it was caught)

Technical resolution (1-2 sentences)

Lesson (1 sentence — what this taught you about payroll precision)
```

**Formula 3: The Bracket Post**
```
Hook: "The 2026 PAYE brackets in one image."

Table/graphic of the brackets

Context: "These are the figures your payroll system should be calculating against."

Linked: "npm install naija-payroll — link in comments."
```

---

## What Not to Post

- **Generic startup advice** — doesn't land in a technical B2B audience
- **"Building in public" without substance** — followers want insights, not progress updates
- **Content that could be anyone** — your specific edge is Nigerian NSITB compliance; stay in that lane
- **Threads about topics outside your domain** — this audience is there for payroll precision, not hot takes on funding rounds

---

## Engagement Strategy

1. **Reply to every comment within 2 hours** — LinkedIn rewards engagement velocity
2. **Comment meaningfully on payroll-adjacent posts** — 2-3 substantive comments on others' content per week
3. **Use polls for low-lift engagement** — "What's your biggest payroll challenge?" — simple, comments heavily
4. **DM follow-up** — if someone comments on your bracket post, DM them the npm link with a one-line summary

---

## The Growth Loop

Each post should serve the loop:

```
Post → Engineers/HR managers save/share → npm downloads increase
npm downloads → credibility → speaking/inbound inquiries → content angles → new posts
```

Don't chase likes. Chase saves. A save from an HR manager at a Nigerian bank is worth more than 50 likes from random connections.

---

*The strategy is simple: post technical depth, post founder stories, stay in your lane, reply fast, chase saves not likes.*
